\begin{sol}
    Counterexample:
    \begin{equation}
        \bff{A} = \begin{bmatrix}
            0 & 2 \\ 
            1 & 1
        \end{bmatrix}
    \end{equation}
    Note, we found this counterexample by factoring:
    \begin{equation}
        \det(\bff{A})\det(\bff{A}+\bff{1})
    \end{equation}
    So we just need to work backwards and find a matrix that isn't invertible, and subtract the identity matrix from it.
\end{sol}
